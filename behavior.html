Cat Behavior & Training

Understanding your cat's behavior is the key to building a strong bond. Cats communicate through body language, routines, and play, and with the right knowledge you can support them every step of the way.

Common Behaviors & What They Mean

  • Purring: Often a sign of comfort and happiness, though sometimes also self-soothing.
  • Scratching: Keeps claws healthy and marks territory—provide scratching posts to protect furniture.
  • Kneading: A leftover kitten behavior that signals trust and affection.
  • Tail flicks: A slow flick may show irritation, while a high upright tail means confidence.

Positive Training

Cats respond best to positive reinforcement. Reward good behavior with treats, play, or affection. Patience and consistency are key—avoid punishment, which can damage trust and increase stress.
